Coursera Content Authoring Tool

In early 2015, Coursera was gaining momentum in moving into a new platform. But one of the main blocker in making the new platform successful was the lack of content authoring tool. Without the tool, Coursera internal team. The design laid the foundations for all consequent authoring tools developed by teams across company.

As the only designer in charge of the project, I designed everything, established admin platform paradigm. and laid down the groundwork for all future authoring tool develoment.

Problem & Opportunity Area


In 2015, through comprehensive user research and journey map, we were able to highlight some key user painpoints and opportunity area, through user research and journey map. We discovered that ---------. The high-level insights immediately helped us identify many opportunities to ease the authoring journey and articulate the benefits of doing so.

The new authoring experience addressed the glaring unbalance between the effort that goes into the course authoring off-line at the beginning of the journey.




The Development


Not only could course authors reach their destination faster (and be happier when they got there), but Coursera could have higher expectations about course quality, if we shaped the journey, provided better tools, and lent our fledgling expertise along the way.

Easy and bite-sized setup stage gives user a sense of progress and easy achievement. Based on the launch date user input during setup stage, Coursera provides recommended milestones during course creation. Built-in timeline with recommended milestones encourages the course creation team to come on-platform earlier on the process. It allows users to conduct beta tests on-platform before the course content is fully developed, so they can collect and incorporate feedback from beta testers early on. These features gave added benefit of providing framework and structures to a process that was considered amorphous and until then.
