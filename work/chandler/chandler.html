The Long Goodbye

One day I was sitting in front of my desk and gazing at my bookshelves. Then I realized that I left most of my books back in Korea but brought all of the Chandler’s books with me. What is it about his work, and his protagonist Phillip Marlowe that speaks to me so strongly? It is simple to say the attraction lies in his prose and the quality of the character, but what would I discover if I went beyond the obvious answers and processed the work using a different language? What would it tell about the story, and about me? Is there a way to decode the limits of a language to put personal subjectivity?

Intrigued by these questions I explored ways to invent a new language and craft a different experience of reading a story.

To promote the intimacy, the text was reduced down to its bare element, eliminating everything else but the few letters that conveys the key emotion of the book. A new font suit was created based on Sabon Roman typeface, using Fontographer.


Distilling the story


The three main story arcs of the plot are each converted into a colour, and as the story evolves and the plots come together in the climax, the colours get mixed to communicate the hightened emotions and interconnected storylines.

The colour overlay was coded and generated in Processing. The entire text of Raymond Chandler’s ‘The long goodbye’ was processed through the algorithm, and I designed, printed, and hand-bound the book myself.
